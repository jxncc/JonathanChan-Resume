Mechanical Engineering

It was a time to remember when I opened my first box of LEGOs. As I tore each LEGO-filled bag apart, I was hit with a strong plastic scent and the sight of a variety of bricks: tiles, plates, and studs. With the instruction manual in hand and LEGO pieces in the other, building the set was the only task left to do. Piece by piece, I found myself getting more drawn into finishing the set until being presented with the finished masterpiece. It was an immediate sense of accomplishment, yet, LEGOs were much more than building by the manual. From TV shows to video games, I spent countless hours replicating my favorite characters. It was an effort inspiring my innovative thinking which immersed me into the world of creativity. I even took my hobby to Youtube where I learned to create my own Lego pieces out of household items: soda cans, fabric, paint, glue.

Eventually, high school approached. While I nervously contemplated over what classes to take, I was recommended to take CAD. Walking into class the first day was nerve-wracking; I was clueless about what CAD was. However, as I was being introduced to the main software applications -AutoCAD and Fusion360- I was hit with nostalgia. I was exposed to an assortment of modeling tools similar to each of the LEGO pieces from my first experience with LEGOs. Naturally, I started to familiarize myself with the tools. From combining the different geometries drawn to forming 3D objects, it reminded me of the task of putting together LEGO pieces.

Over time, I began mastering the tools where I learned to create functioning objects: cars with spinning wheels, boxes with screw-on lids. With the projects in CAD class, I was always able to incorporate my thought and skills into creating unique models. Outside of school, the creative nature I’ve built from LEGOs and CAD taught me to accomplish tasks not by taking ordinary steps, but through the guidance of my thoughts. Thoughts of which display individuality and allows me to perceive everything I do differently than my peers.
